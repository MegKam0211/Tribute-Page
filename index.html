Agatha Christie

Novelist, short story writer, playwright, poet, memoirist

Dame Agatha Mary Clarissa Christie, DBE (Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire)

b. Sep 15 1890 d. Jan 12 1976

A timeline of Agatha Christie's life:

  • Sep 15 1890 - Agatha Christie is born
  • Dec 26 1914 - Agatha marries Archibald "Archie" Christie before he was sent to France to fight in the First World War.
  • From October 1914 to May 1915, then from June 1916 to September 1918 - She worked in the Town Hall Red Cross Hospital, Torquay, first as a nurse and then after qualifying as an apothecaries' assistant, she worked as a dispenser
  • Oct 1920 - Agatha Christie publishes her first detective novel, The Mysterious Affair at Styles. Introducing the character, Hercule Poirot
  • Jan 1922 - Agatha Christie publishes The Secret Adversary. Featuring a new detective couple Tommy and Tuppence.
  • In 1922 - The Christies joined an around-the-world promotional tour for the British Empire Exhibition, led by Major Ernest Belcher. They learned to surf prone in South Africa; then, in Waikiki, they were among the first Britons to surf standing up. When they returned to England, they bought a house in Sunningdale, Berkshire, which they renamed Styles after the mansion in Christie's first detective novel.
  • August 1926 - Archie asked Christie for a divorce. He had fallen in love with Nancy Neele, a friend of Major Belcher.
  • 3 December 1926 - Late that evening, Christie disappeared from their home. The following morning, her car, a Morris Cowley
  • 14 December 1926 - She was located at the Swan Hydropathic Hotel in Harrogate, Yorkshire, registered as Mrs Tressa Neele (the surname of her husband's lover) from "Capetown S.A." (South Africa).
  • April 1928 - Her divorce from Archie is finalized.
  • 1928 - Christie left England and took the (Simplon) Orient Express to Istanbul and then to Baghdad.
  • February 1930 - In Iraq, she became friends with archaeologist Leonard Woolley and his wife, who invited her to return to their dig. On that second trip, she met an archaeologist, thirteen years her junior, Max Mallowan.
  • September 1930 - Christie and Mallowan married in Edinburgh.
  • April 1930Christie's first novel under the pseudonym of Mary Westmacott, called Giant's Bread.
  • 1934 - They bought Winterbrook House in Winterbrook, a hamlet near Wallingford.
  • From 1971 to 1974 - Christie's health began to fail, but she continued to write. Her last novel was Postern of Fate in 1973
  • 1975 and 1976 - Christie wrote Sleeping Murder and Curtain during World War II to be published after her death.

"The impossible could not have happened, therefore the impossible must be possible in spite of appearances."

-- Agatha Christie (Murder on the Orient Express, 1934)
